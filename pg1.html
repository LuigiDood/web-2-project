500-1000 words Clair Obscur: Expedition 33 is a rare breed of RPGs you'll find that hits the sweet spot in every way you can think of. Story? Top-notch. Characters? Fledged-out and interesting. Combat? Enjoyable and unique. Everything put into this game has a soul of its own that makes you enjoy every moment you play it. This Game of the Year contender has been one of the biggest highlights of the RPG genre of the decade so far.

Entering a ruined world plagued by death, in the French city of Lumiere, your party of Expedition 33, compiled of of brave adventurers and those experiencing their last year of their life, must make the yearly and perilous joruney to
to stop the Paintress, who every year erases everyone past a certain age from existence, and end the cycle of death. The game immediately gives a good first impression, both in terms of graphics and story. The bizzare fantasy world that unfolds in front of you, and the way the story ties into it never loses its charm throughout the game.
