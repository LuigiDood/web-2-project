Persona 5 Strikers

I can't necessarily say I've always been a big fan of spin-off games. They often get stuck as plain, unoriginal, like another low-end game with another IP slapped onto it to make sure it sells. But somehow, Persona 5 Strikers defies all expectations I held for a spin-off game. Hack-and-slash musuo games are something I don't have a lot of experience with, so going in I had concerns on things like the gameplay loop and if it would hold up well as a spin-off sequel to Persona 5, and it absolutely did. I could make an entire separate article on what I find bad about P5's other spin-off, Persona 5 Tactica, but for now the focus should be on what Strikers does well. It really feels like an authentic follow up to P5, with a story that you can tell had effort put into and surprisingly holds up eerily well today. The gameplay can admittedly get repetitive sometimes, but I found generally even while playing at the hardest difficulty that the only parts I found repetitive were early boss fights. And of course, a spin-off can't feel like an authentic sequel without implementing game mechanics of the original, which Strikers does very well, as it feels about as true to the original as the developers could have made it.

Everything about the game feels appropriately inspired yet unique from the original, all fitting in in a manner that makes it nostalgic but still enjoyable. Atlus didn't fall into the trap of trying to give the game too much of its own identity, which is something many spin-offs will fall into. I also appreciate the challenge given at times, as some parts can be especially tough until you discover a good strategy. This is also where I think the aspect of building an ideal team comes in. While some team members are clearly better than others (Sorry Ryuji), there is still never a set roster that is essential in order to win, especially given the inconsistency on whether my team cares about avoiding dying in one hit or not, regardless of who you bring. Still, the battle mechanics are implemented in such a creative style that is original yet not too outlandishly trying to be different.

Of course, no game is perfect, and I had my biggest issues with repetitiveness in the gameplay loop at some points, as well as some inconsistencies and aspects that are poorly implemented and made unnecessarily complex and confusing compared to the original. But outside of these issues, likely restrictions from being limited to a side project, or the awkwardness of trying a new genre, I give this game high praise for the value it brings for a non-mainline game. Overall an 8/10 experience that I would recommend to anyone who enjoyed the original Persona 5.
